Introductory Psychology Quiz

Basic Concept Review & Foundations of Mental Health

Part 1: Common Mental Health Conditions
Question 1

A person experiences a persistent low mood, loss of interest in activities they used to enjoy, constant tiredness, and changes in sleep patterns lasting for several weeks. What common mental health condition best describes this?

Expected Answer: Depression (Major Depressive Disorder).

Question 2

Someone experiences sudden, unexpected bursts of extreme fear accompanied by a pounding heart, sweating, and shortness of breath that peak within minutes. What are these episodes called?

Expected Answer: Panic Attacks (Panic Disorder).

Question 3

A person experiences extreme mood swings that cycle between periods of high energy, talking very fast, and grand plans (mania), followed by periods of deep sadness and low motivation. What condition involves these severe shifts?

Expected Answer: Bipolar Disorder.

Question 4

An individual feels intense, overwhelming nervousness and self-consciousness when meeting new people or speaking in public because they are terrified of being judged or embarrassed. What specific anxiety presentation is this?

Expected Answer: Social Anxiety (Social Phobia).

Question 5

After experiencing a highly stressful, scary, or dangerous life event, a person struggles with frequent nightmares about the event, feels constantly on edge, and avoids anything that reminds them of it. What condition does this look like?

Expected Answer: Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD).

Question 6

A person experiences recurring, unwanted thoughts (like fear of germs) and feels driven to perform repetitive behaviors or routines over and over (like washing hands twenty times) to make the anxiety go away. What is this called?

Expected Answer: Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD).

Question 7

A person is constantly worried about everyday things like their health, work, or chores, even when there is no clear reason to worry. The anxiety lasts for months and makes it hard to relax. What condition is this?

Expected Answer: Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD).

Part 2: Basic Professional & Communication Skills
Question 8

When a helper listens carefully to someone without interrupting, pays attention to their feelings, and states back what they heard to show they understand, what basic communication skill are they using?

Expected Answer: Active Listening.

Question 9

If a professional rules that they will not share or talk about a client's private stories with outside individuals or friends, what fundamental ethical concept are they following?

Expected Answer: Confidentiality.

Question 10

What is the professional term for setting clear, healthy limits on relationship topics, personal talk, and meeting times to keep the helper-client relationship safe and focused?

Expected Answer: Professional Boundaries.

Part 3: Basic Approaches to Change
Question 11

Which popular style of talking therapy focuses directly on helping people notice how their negative thoughts influence their feelings and actions, teaching them to swap unhelpful thoughts for balanced ones?

Expected Answer: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT).

Question 12

When someone works through a fear (like a fear of heights) by slowly and safely facing it step-by-step under calm conditions until it no longer scares them, what basic behavioral technique is being used?

Expected Answer: Exposure Therapy (or Desensitization).

Question 13

What is the term for actions, habits, or strategies (such as deep breathing, journaling, or regular exercise) that a person uses to manage stress and keep themselves feeling healthy?

Expected Answer: Coping Mechanisms (or Self-Care Strategies).

Question 14

What is the basic psychological term for an automatic defense action where a person pushes a highly uncomfortable or stressful memory entirely out of their conscious awareness so they don't have to face it?

Expected Answer: Repression.

Question 15

What classic term describes the strong sense of trust, mutual respect, and collaborative connection built between a helper and a client that allows them to work well together?

Expected Answer: Therapeutic Alliance (or Rapport).
